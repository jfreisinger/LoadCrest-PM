# SK-005: Common Coupling Discovery

## Purpose

Surfaces genuine points of shared interest, mission alignment, technical conviction, and commercial overlap between LoadCrest and a specific partner, customer, or investor — *before* any in-person engagement. Provides the facilitator with the raw material for Group Flow's familiarity and shared-goal conditions. Outputs feed directly into [SK-004](SK-004-group-flow-facilitation.md) session design.

The core premise: people who know *why they actually agree* collaborate at a qualitatively higher level than people who show up hoping to find agreement. This skill makes the implicit explicit.

---

## When to Use

- Before any first or second meeting with a prospective partner, customer, or investor
- When preparing a workshop or working session where relationship depth matters to the outcome
- When a prior engagement felt transactional or stalled — to reset the relational foundation
- When onboarding a new team member who will be the primary contact for a specific external party

---

## Coupling Dimensions

Common coupling exists across five dimensions. The goal is to find at least two genuine overlaps per counterparty — the more specific and non-obvious, the more powerful.

| Dimension | What to Look For | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Mission** | Shared beliefs about what the world should look like — energy transition, AI infrastructure, national security, grid resilience | The deepest coupling; activates *blending egos* and *equal participation* in Group Flow |
| **Market** | Shared conviction about where a market is going — AI compute growth, off-grid deployment, 800VDC adoption | Creates strategic alignment; reduces adversarial framing |
| **Technical** | Overlapping technical bets — power electronics topology, control system philosophy, materials science | Enables deep working-session collaboration; activates *familiarity* condition |
| **Commercial** | Aligned business model preferences, shared customer segments, complementary revenue structures | Reduces friction in partnership and commercial structuring conversations |
| **Values** | How the other party makes decisions, treats people, and talks about its work — speed vs. rigor, openness vs. IP-first | Predicts how collaboration will feel over time; surfaces potential friction early |

## Steps

### Step 1: Research the Counterparty

Spend 30–60 minutes in focused research before drafting any coupling profile. Prioritize:

1. **Mission language** — What words do they use to describe what they're for, not just what they do? Look for mission statements, founder essays, investor letters, keynote talks. Note verbatim phrases.

2. **Strategic bets** — What technical or market bets are they making that are non-obvious? What would be true if they're right? What would be true if they're wrong?

3. **Constraints and pressures** — What does this party *need* right now? What's blocking them? What would a win look like in the next 6–12 months? (Job postings and recent hires are often the most honest signal here.)

4. **Network and relationships** — Who do they trust? Who introduced us? What shared relationships exist that could accelerate familiarity?

5. **How they've talked about us or our space** — Any public statements about AI power infrastructure, 800VDC, microgrids, or competitors?

---

### Step 2: Map Coupling Against LoadCrest

For each coupling dimension, identify the specific overlap and rate its strength:

| Strength | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Strong** | Explicit, documented, independently arrived-at alignment — the other party has said it publicly in their own words |
| **Probable** | Logical inference from their market position, technical bets, or customer base — likely true but not yet confirmed |
| **Candidate** | A hypothesis worth testing in the session — may be coupling, may not be; open it as a question rather than stating it as fact |

Do not overstate coupling. A forced or manufactured overlap will be felt immediately and damages trust faster than no coupling at all.

---

### Step 3: Draft the Coupling Profile

Produce a one-page coupling profile with the following structure:

```
COUNTERPARTY: [Name]
TYPE: [Partner | Customer | Investor]
DATE: [YYYY-MM-DD]
PREPARED BY: [Name]

STRONG COUPLINGS
- [Dimension]: [Specific overlap — one sentence, with evidence]

PROBABLE COUPLINGS
- [Dimension]: [Specific overlap — one sentence, with inference basis]

CANDIDATE QUESTIONS (test these in session)
- [Dimension]: [Open question that would confirm or disconfirm this as coupling]

POTENTIAL FRICTION POINTS
- [Dimension]: [Where our interests or values may diverge — be honest]

OPENING FRAME
One sentence naming the shared interest for use in the session open (see SK-004 Step 8):
"Everyone in this room [shared mission/market/conviction]..."
```

---

### Step 4: Validate with the Team

Before any external meeting, share the coupling profile with at least one other LoadCrest team member. Ask:

- Does anything feel forced or inaccurate?
- Are there coupling points we've experienced in prior interactions that aren't captured here?
- Are there friction points we've avoided naming that should be in the profile?

This validation step is itself a Group Flow moment — surface it and use it.

---

### Step 5: Brief the Facilitator

If the session facilitator is not the person who built the coupling profile, transfer it with a 5-minute verbal brief. The facilitator needs to *feel* the coupling, not just read it. They will use it in:

- Step 8 of SK-004 (stating the shared interest)
- Redirecting the conversation when it becomes transactional
- Recognizing when the session has found genuine common ground vs. polite agreement

---

## Counterparty-Specific Guidance

### Partners (Dyna Power, IONATE, VEIR, Sandia, USAF)

Partners carry the most coupling potential because they have independently made similar technical bets. Prioritize **Technical** and **Mission** dimensions. The key coupling question for any partner is:

> *"What would have to be true about the future of power infrastructure for both of us to win?"*

If you can both answer that question honestly and arrive at similar answers, you have deep coupling. Build from there.

| Partner | Known or probable coupling |
|---------|---------------------------|
| **Dyna Power** | 800VDC as the right standard; AI load dynamics requiring new converter approaches; commercial DC/DC market growth |
| **IONATE** | SST as the right H3 architecture; displacing LFTs at grid-scale; bidirectional power flow as a commercial opportunity |
| **VEIR** | High-density power transmission as a limiting factor in AI infrastructure density |
| **Sandia National Labs** | 800VDC microgrid research origins (shared founding context); grid resilience as national security |
| **U.S. Air Force** | Resilient, off-grid power for mission-critical compute; validated by FOAK deployment |

---

### Customers (Neocloud Providers, AI Factory Developers)

Customers are not looking for a mission partner — they are looking for someone who understands their problem better than they do. Prioritize **Market** and **Commercial** dimensions. The coupling question:

> *"What keeps you up at night about your power situation, and what would change if it were solved?"*

Listen for the specific language they use. Mirror it back before presenting LoadCrest's framing. Coupling here is built by demonstrating that you have already thought about their problem more rigorously than they expected.

**Common coupling signals in neocloud conversations:**
- Frustration with utility interconnection timelines ("waiting 3 years for a transformer")
- CapEx pressure on power infrastructure relative to compute spend
- Operational reliability concerns at scale (one power event = lost GPU-hours = customer SLA breach)
- Interest in off-grid or pre-grid deployment as a competitive differentiator
- Curiosity about 800VDC but uncertainty about who to trust to deliver it

---

### Investors

Investors have a thesis, and your job is to find where your work is the strongest expression of that thesis — not to convince them to adopt a new one. Prioritize **Mission** and **Market** dimensions. The coupling question:

> *"What does the world look like in 10 years if the bets you've made in AI infrastructure are right — and where does power sit in that picture?"*

**Common coupling signals by investor thesis:**

| Investor Thesis | LoadCrest Coupling Point |
|----------------|------------------------|
| AI infrastructure is the defining infrastructure buildout of the decade | Power is the binding constraint on AI compute — LoadCrest removes it |
| Energy transition requires new grid architectures | 800VDC microgrids are the new building block; LoadCrest is the control layer |
| Deep tech / hard tech with durable moats | Sentinel is a novel embedded control algorithm; not replicable from first principles without years of field data |
| National security / dual-use technology | FOAK deployment with USAF; Sandia pedigree; resilient off-grid compute |
| Pay-for-performance / outcome-based infrastructure | EaaS model aligns LoadCrest revenue with customer uptime — identical model to what works in healthcare and energy |

**Important:** Do not treat investor meetings as a pitch. Treat them as a discovery session where you are trying to understand whether their thesis and your work are genuinely aligned. Forced coupling with the wrong investor creates structural problems later.

| Output | Destination | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Coupling profile (one page) | `meeting-notes/` or shared drive | Archived for continuity; updated after each engagement |

| Candidate questions | Session agenda | Surfaces unconfirmed coupling hypotheses during the session |
| Friction points noted | Internal team awareness | Not surfaced externally unless it becomes a blocker |

---

## After the Session: Update the Profile

Within 24 hours of any session, the facilitator or note-taker should update the coupling profile with:
- Which couplings were confirmed, disproved, or deepened
- New coupling discovered in session
- Friction points that surfaced
- The counterparty's own language and framing — capture it verbatim where possible

The coupling profile becomes the institutional memory of the relationship. It compounds over time.
